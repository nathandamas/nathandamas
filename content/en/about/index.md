# About

Nathan Damas Antonio is a researcher in GIScience and geospatial technologies, with a background in Cartographic and Land Survey Engineering and Geodetic Sciences. His work brings together collaborative cartography, OpenStreetMap, spatial and spatio-temporal analysis, semantic interoperability, spatial data infrastructures, photogrammetry, LiDAR, UAV-based data, and 3D cadastre.

His academic and professional trajectory combines research, teaching, and applied technical work. He is currently a Ph.D. candidate in Geodetic Sciences at the Federal University of Paraná, has worked as a visiting researcher at Heidelberg University, and has been involved in projects related to territorial data management, spatial database structuring, interoperability, and geospatial automation.

He has experience with Python, PostGIS, QGIS, GeoServer, GeoNode, spatial ETL, metadata management, geospatial standards, and collaborative geographic data. His broader research interests include OpenStreetMap, volunteered geographic information, urban mobility, spatial data quality, and emerging computational approaches such as NLP and large language models in geospatial contexts.
